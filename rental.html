FAQ

The maximum sellable capacity for events hosted in Convocation Hall is 1,650 guests. However, depending on your event configuration, the maximum capacity will vary:
  • General Admission – No Screens = 1,650
  • General Admission – With Screens = 1,450
  • Tiered Seating – No Screens = 1,618
  • Tiered Seating – With Screens = 1,418
Event rental costs vary for the use of Convocation Hall, determined based on your requirements, such as number of attendees and ticketing style etc. We will provide you with a tailored estimate once we understand the full scope of your event requirements
We require all events in Convocation Hall to be ticketed from one source. We do not provide ticket authenticating devices (scanners), however we are happy to provide the staffing to operate the devices as supplied by your ticketing provider.
Unfortunately, we cannot permit events to sell on an assigned seating basis. Event tickets can be sold as ‘General Admission” or broken down into a Maximum of 2 ticketing tiers (Main Hall – Floor & Rise, Balconies – 1st & 2nd). For events utilizing tiered seating, wristbands must be provided by the client for all those seated in the Main Hall
Yes, we do allow merchandise sales. However, please note there is a fee of $200 and the following will apply:
  • For events over 700 attendees, merchandise must be located outside of the building and vendor must provide own battery operated lighting (There is no power available)
  • If under 700 attendees, IKL Stairwell may be permitted for merchandise sales.
Unfortunately, we are unable to support will call ticket pickups or onsite ticket sales. Tickets must either be printable, accessible online or mailed directly to attendees.
We are happy to make Convocation Hall available from 7:00 – 23:00. For any extension of time beyond the 23:00 caused by the licensee, an additional charge of $1,000 per hour (or part thereof) will apply, in addition to the appropriate staffing costs.
We are able to accommodate a maximum of 5 wheelchairs parked on the floor level of Convocation Hall. There are an additional 228 seats available on the floor level of the Hall for guests who are transferable or do not need a wheelchair spot.
  • 32 seats will be held back on the floor level for all events to accommodate unplanned accessibility requests
  • Please also note, the University does not permit event promoters to sell identified accessible seats at a premium rate.
Please note the use of the Universities name, branding or logo is not permitted to be utilized on external event promotional materials.
Yes, Popup banners or A-Frame signage are permitted on campus. Please note no signage can be affixed to the building or staked in the ground anywhere on Campus. Our Ambassadors are happy to assist in directing your guests.
Unfortunately, Convocation Hall is not licensed and does not have the facilities to host a reception. There are some spaces on campus Hart House, Rotman, Faculty Club, Chestnut
Unfortunately, we are unable to provide coat check or bag check for events at Convocation Hall. We are able to check in large oversized bags, car seats, strollers or mobility devices that would otherwise cause a fire egress concern.
We require a minimum of 1 hour between doors opening and event show time. For events with 1,600+ attendees, we recommend doors are opened 1.5 hours to ensure guests are comfortably able to be seated before show time.
Please note, all event lineups will be managed outside of the venue – there is no foyer space to accommodate internal waiting area
We are able to accommodate many stage configurations to suit your event needs. However, we do require that at least one of the accessible ramps remains clear at all times.
There is a room within the basement of Convocation Hall (Room 23) available for your use with your rental. Please note this room is not accessible and does require stair access. On evenings and weekends we can reserve a Green Room in the adjoining accessible Simcoe Hall building.
Please note, Green Rooms are not licensed and do not have private washroom facilities
A conference WIFI account can be arranged for your staff/guests for a flat fee of $200. Please note we will need 5 business days’ notice to accommodate a request for WIFI.
Unfortunately, there is no loading dock at Convocation Hall. We do have a curb cut at the accessible entrance off of Kings College Circle. We are happy to discuss the load in/out process in more details specifically relating to your needs.
Yes, we can reserve a maximum of 100 seats for VIP attendees. We will require a guest list of VIPs and/or a way for the VIPs to be identified by our front of house staff.
Yes, we are happy to discuss your requirements with our Transportation Services team to make arrangements for reserved parking. Please note additional costs will be applicable.
Yes, we are happy to discuss your requirements with our Transportation Services team to make arrangements to accommodate your bus drop off. Please note additional costs will be applicable.
Convocation Hall can only be booked by a company or organization, unfortunately we cannot accommodate individual personal rentals.
After we have reviewed your event request, a Contract and Estimate will be provided. We will require a signed copy of the contract along with a non-refundable deposit in order to confirm your event rental.
Yes, a comprehensive general liability insurance policy satisfactory to the University with inclusive limits of one million dollars per occurrence, which shall contain a cross-liability clause and name “The Governing Council of the University of Toronto” as an additional named insured will be required prior to your event rental date
