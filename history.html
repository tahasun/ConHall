Established in 1907

The University of Toronto Alumni Association raised more than $50,000 (nearly $1 million in today's dollars) for its construction, an amount that was matched by the Province.

The Alumni Association wanted to honour the University degree properly with this new magnificant Convocation Hall. Moreover, the Hall was meant to provide a space for students to congregate for social and academic matters.

1900's

650 students Graduated in 1906 in Convocation Hall when it was under construction

1920's

1923 Convocation garden party

The University of Toronto Students' Union hosted Garden Parties following the conferring of degrees.

1 in 4 of the graduating class were women

1950's

U of T Symphony Orchestra rehearsing at Convocation Hall

The UofT Orchestra and Chorus gave two concerts in Convocation Hall, which were largely attended by members of staff and student body of the University

1960's

Protest Against the Vietnam War in front of Convocation Hall

In the fall of 1968 R. Buckminster Fuller was invited to deliver the first of a series of annual lectures. After spending some time with the students and talking about his urban renewal proposals for downtown Toronto, Dr. Fuller gave an extended dissertation to a very large audience in convocation Hall.

1970's

The Alumni Association wanted to honour the University degree properly with this new magnificant Convocation Hall. Moreover, the Hall was meant to provide a space for students to congregate for social and academic matters.

1980's

Pierre Elliott Trudeau reminisced on his sixteen rows as Prime Minister in front of a capacity crowd at Convocation Hall in 1984. Trudeau participated in a question and answer session sponsored by the Young Liberals of University of Toronto.

1990's

Members of the Toronto region of Canadian Union of Public Employees which includes UofT’s teaching assistants, library workers and maintenance employees, gathered at Convocation Hall to discuss and vote to stop Bill 136.

2000's

In 2006, a major restoration and refurbishment was undertaken by E.R.A Architects with funding from the alumni association. Work entailed refurbishing seats, restoration of grandeur of the circular foyer including decorative finishes, historical millwork, lighting installations, installing accessible washrooms and a fresh coat of paint, and restoration of the historic pipe organ—the fifth largest in Toronto
